Security Project

Network Recon & Packet Analysis

Reconnaissance and packet analysis to identify exposed services and abnormal traffic patterns.

Kali Linux Nmap Wireshark SOC Monitoring
Scenario

Performed reconnaissance on a lab network to simulate the information-gathering phase of a security assessment. The goal was to identify potential attack surfaces and practice recognizing traffic patterns relevant to SOC monitoring and threat detection.

Tools Used

Nmap was used on Kali Linux to scan the lab network for active hosts, open ports, and running services. Wireshark was run simultaneously to capture live traffic and observe network behavior during the scan in real time.

What I Analyzed

Identified exposed services and open ports that could represent attack vectors. Detected abnormal traffic patterns in the packet capture — including scan signatures and service probes — that would trigger alerts in a SIEM environment.

Decision Made

Documented exposed services with recommended hardening actions for each. Identified which findings would be flagged as suspicious in a SIEM and defined the appropriate SOC response for each traffic pattern — mapping detections to realistic analyst workflows.
